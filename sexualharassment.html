Definition

Sexual harassment is any unwelcome or inappropriate behavior of a sexual nature that makes someone uncomfortable, embarrassed, or threatened. It can include verbal, physical, or digital actions that create a hostile environment.

Types

Different types of sexual harassment:

  • Quid pro quo (offering benefits in exchange for sexual favors)
  • Hostile environment (creating a hostile or intimidating atmosphere)
  • Sexual coercion (pressuring someone into sexual activities)

    • Examples

    Examples of sexual harassment include:

  • Unwanted advances
  • lewd comments or jokes
  • displaying explicit materials
  • Touching without consent
  • using authority to make sexual demands

    • Recognize it

    Recognizing sexual harassment involves noticing behaviors like unwanted advances, physical contact without consent, creating a hostile environment through explicit comments or jokes, power imbalances leading to pressure, online harassment, repeated unwanted behavior, and trusting your instincts if something feels wrong. It's important to be aware of these signs and take appropriate action if you or someone else experiences sexual harassment.

    Reporting Procedures

    If you experience or witness sexual harassment, report it to a designated authority or HR department. Provide detailed information about the incident, and follow the organization's reporting procedures to ensure a prompt and thorough investigation.

    Legal Implications

    Sexual harassment is illegal and can result in serious consequences, including disciplinary actions, civil lawsuits, and criminal charges. Know your legal rights and seek support if you experience sexual harassment.

    Case stories

    Sarah worked at a tech startup where her manager, Tom, would often make suggestive comments about her appearance during team meetings. Despite Sarah's discomfort and attempts to change the subject, Tom continued with the remarks. Eventually, Sarah reported the behavior to HR, leading to an investigation and Tom receiving disciplinary action.

    Sarah

    Alex was a graduate student in a lab where a senior researcher, Maya, made unwelcome advances and touched Alex inappropriately under the guise of mentoring. Alex felt trapped and unsure of how to address the situation until seeking advice from a trusted faculty member, who helped Alex file a complaint with the university's Title IX office.

    Alex

    Emily, a gaming enthusiast and streamer, faced relentless harassment from a viewer who sent sexually explicit messages and threats during her live streams. Emily banned the user from her channel and reported the harassment to the streaming platform, which took action to block the user and improve moderation tools.

    Emily

    David, a male employee at a retail store, faced repeated sexual advances from his female supervisor, Lisa. She made inappropriate comments about his appearance, touched him without consent, and pressured him into going on dates. David felt uncomfortable but feared backlash. With a coworker's support, he reported Lisa to HR, leading to disciplinary action. David's action raised awareness about sexual harassment against men in the workplace.

    David
